Nepal Earthquake Data Explorer

What is it ?

Nepal Earthquake Data Explorer a.k.a. NEDa is a scholarship project that aims to provide and present data of the 2015 Nepal Earthquake and its aftershocks. Among the data provided is: damages made by the earthquake and aftershocks; statistics about partially and fully destroyed households; efforts made to reconstruct collapsed buildings and many more.

Who made it ?

Three devoted students. See the Contact page.

What did you use ?

During the development period we used many suites of frameworks and libraries both on front-end and back-end. Those include:

Front-end:

  • Bootstrap: http://getbootstrap.com/
  • JQuery: https://jquery.com/
  • Google Charts API: https://developers.google.com/chart/
  • Google Maps API: https://developers.google.com/maps/

Back-end

  • Java Spring Boot: https://projects.spring.io/spring-boot/
  • Apache Maven: https://maven.apache.org/ for library dependencies

Also lots and lots of Stack Overflow.
